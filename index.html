About Me

A PEANUT BUTTER AND JELLY SANDWICH?!

That's right, a peanut butter and jelly sandwich was all it took for me to be hooked on coding.

I've always loved logic at it's most basic. Starting in sixth grade, I used to ask teachers for more logic equations. Do you remember those sheets that had a grid and said something crazy like:

"A man is taking his prized pineapple, leopard, llama, and pet leviathan (an unique creature that eats llamas and leopards unless a pineapple is present) to market. A ship awaits him to cross a river, but he can only take one item at a time. How can the man cross the river, without the llama eating the pineapple, the leopard eating the llama, and the leviathan eating the llama and the leopard?"

When COVID hit over the spring, I purchased a programming book for high school students. For fun! I'd always wanted to learn programming and loved every minute of this book. The first programming assignment was to make a formula for a peanut butter and jelly sandwich. The task instantly threw me back to middle school days and my embarrassing sixth grade self.

The excitement made me purchase more books and play around in JavaScript, HTML and Python. I also enrolled in a full stack programming boot camp with the University of New Hampshire starting November 2020.

My career thus far has been accomplished. Each day I practice leadership v. management, mindfulness, and truly serve my team. As a leader, I'm here to work for my team. My co-workers would call me diligent, accurate, honest, open, hard-working, and has high standards. For myself and for the teams I work with.

I'm looking to combine my finance, business, and operational experience to truly blossom as a software developer.
